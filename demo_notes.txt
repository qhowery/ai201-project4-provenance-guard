────────────────────────────────────────
INTRO (~30 sec)
────────────────────────────────────────
Show: README → Architecture diagram.

Say:
"Hi, I'm Quonlee Howery. This is Provenance Guard — a backend that classifies
submitted text as likely human or AI-written and returns a plain-language
transparency label.

The design uses two complementary signals, not one. Signal 1 is a Groq LLM
classifier that catches semantic tells like filler phrases. Signal 2 is
stylometrics — burstiness and punctuation entropy — that catches structural
uniformity. Neither signal is trusted alone.

Scores fuse 60/40 into a confidence value. If the signals disagree by more
than 0.40, the system forces uncertain instead of over-flagging a human writer.
Every decision is logged, creators can appeal, and submit is rate limited.

I'll walk through three submissions that hit human, uncertain, and high-AI —
then the audit log, an appeal, and rate limiting."
